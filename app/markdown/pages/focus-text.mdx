Working with some agencies can mean spending the first few meetings explaining how your sector operates: the funding restrictions, the reporting requirements, the trustee oversight. It can be exhausting, and you may end up paying for their learning curve.

I've worked inside the charity sector. I know that your website probably sits in a very long list of priorities. I know that your budget is a fixed, precious thing — not a starting point for negotiation.

I chose to work in this sector deliberately. It's not a sideline. It's why I set up Big Pixel in the first place. I'm a member of Amnesty International, I support digital rights and LGBTQ+ equality, and the organisations I work with tend to reflect those values.

### How I can help organisations like yours:

- **Websites** that clearly communicate your mission and are actually easy to keep updated
- **Donation pages** and forms that work properly on every device, because a broken donate button is a real problem
- **Newsletter** sign-ups and CRM integration that your team can actually manage
- Genuine **WCAG accessibility** compliance — important both for your audience and for demonstrating best practice to funders
- **Ongoing support** and training so your team feels confident long after launch
